Privacy Policy

Effective Date: May 1, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Amicus MCP ("the Software"), developed by Amicus Advisory Group LLC ("we," "us," or "our"), handles information when you use our QuickBooks Online integration tool.

1. Information We Access

When you authorize the Software to connect to your QuickBooks Online account, it may access:

  • Company information (name, address, fiscal year settings)
  • Financial records (chart of accounts, journal entries, invoices, bills, deposits, purchases)
  • Contact records (customers, vendors, employees)
  • Financial reports (profit and loss, balance sheet, cash flow, general ledger, trial balance, aged receivables and payables)

2. How We Use Information

The Software accesses your QuickBooks data solely to:

  • Display financial information to you, the authorized user
  • Perform accounting operations you explicitly request (creating journal entries, updating records, generating reports)
  • Facilitate financial analysis and reporting for your business

3. Data Storage and Transmission

The Software operates locally on your machine. Specifically:

  • No cloud storage: Your QuickBooks data is not stored on any external server or cloud platform operated by us
  • No third-party sharing: Your data is never transmitted to, sold to, or shared with any third party
  • Local credentials: OAuth tokens and API credentials are stored exclusively in a local environment file on your machine
  • Direct API communication: All data flows directly between your machine and Intuit's QuickBooks Online API servers over encrypted HTTPS connections

4. Authentication

The Software uses Intuit's OAuth 2.0 protocol for authentication. During the initial setup, you authorize the Software through Intuit's secure login page. We never see, store, or have access to your Intuit username or password. The Software stores only the OAuth refresh token, which is kept locally on your machine.

5. Data Retention

We do not retain any of your QuickBooks data. Data is accessed in real time when you use the Software and is not persisted beyond the active session. If you disconnect the Software from your QuickBooks account, no residual data remains on our end because no data was ever stored externally.

6. Security

We implement the following security measures:

  • All API communications use TLS/HTTPS encryption
  • OAuth tokens are stored locally and never exposed over a network
  • Write operations (creating, updating, or deleting records) require explicit user authorization before execution
  • The Software runs locally via standard input/output — it does not expose any network ports or endpoints during normal operation

7. Your Rights

You may at any time:

  • Revoke the Software's access to your QuickBooks account through Intuit's app management settings
  • Delete locally stored credentials by removing the environment configuration file
  • Request information about how your data is handled by contacting us

8. Children's Privacy

The Software is a business accounting tool and is not intended for use by individuals under 18 years of age. We do not knowingly collect information from children.
